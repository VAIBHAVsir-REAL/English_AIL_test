Travel Guidelines - Karnataka

🛂 Inter-District & Border Rules

No E-pass needed for travel within Karnataka, including Coorg. If coming from Kerala or Maharashtra, carry a negative RT-PCR COVID test (≤ 72 hours old). 14-days home quarantine expected—can end early if a second test is negative after 7 days.

Expect random checkposts (mostly at night). No fees unless documents are incorrect.

🚍 Transport & Checkpoints

KSRTC buses (AC/Volvo) run at ~50% capacity. Expect temperature checks and mask checks.

Private vehicles may be stopped at checkposts. Valid ID and RC may be asked. Sometimes a small ₹20 fee.

Avoid late-night travel due to mist, fewer vehicles, and remote checks.

😷 Health & Safety Protocols

Masks, hand hygiene, and 2 m distancing are mandatory in public places, hotels, and tourist spots.

Thermal checks at transport hubs and hotels. Symptomatic travelers are denied entry.

Self-declaration (travel history, ID) required. Hotels keep this info. Aarogya Setu app is advised.

🏨 Accommodation & Homestays

Only asymptomatic guests allowed. Luggage is sanitized. No room visitors, minimum staff contact.

Homestays must be registered (max 5–6 rooms, owner must live there). Unregistered ones may be shut.

🏛️ Tourist Attractions & Police

CCTV, “tourist mitras” (tourist police), and helplines exist but coverage is limited.

Police may restrict access to major sites. Rely on local guides for timings & tips.
